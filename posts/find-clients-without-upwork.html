How to Find Clients Without Using Upwork or Fiverr

Discover effective strategies to attract clients independently and build your own freelance network.

When most beginners think about freelancing, platforms like Upwork and Fiverr are often the first places they turn to. While these sites can help you get started, they’re also saturated, competitive, and often take a cut of your earnings. If you're serious about building a sustainable freelance business, learning how to find clients outside of these platforms can give you more control, better pay, and long-term relationships.

1. Use Social Media to Showcase Your Skills

Social media is one of the most powerful tools for freelancers today — and it’s completely free. Platforms like LinkedIn, Twitter (X), Facebook, and even Instagram allow you to post your work, share insights, and connect directly with potential clients.

  • Post regularly about your niche or service (e.g., tips, advice, or results).
  • Share your portfolio or before/after project examples.
  • Engage in discussions within groups and on posts.
  • Use relevant hashtags like #webdesign, #virtualassistant, etc.

2. Cold Pitching — the Right Way

Cold pitching means directly reaching out to businesses or individuals who might benefit from your service. It might sound scary, but when done professionally, it works.

Steps:

  • Identify ideal clients (e.g., coaches, bloggers, small shops).
  • Research their business and identify a need.
  • Send a short, personalized message or email with how you can help.
"Hi [Name], I love what you're doing with [Business Name]. I noticed your website could benefit from a refresh to improve loading speed and user experience. I’m a freelance web designer and would love to help you make those improvements. Let me know if you're interested in a quick call."

3. Build a Personal Website or Portfolio

Having your own website makes you look more professional. It also gives clients a place to see your work and contact you directly — no middleman.

Include:

  • Short intro/homepage
  • Services you offer
  • Portfolio or samples
  • Testimonials (if any)
  • Contact form or email

4. Tap into Freelance Communities and Forums

There are online spaces where freelancers and clients connect. These include:

  • Reddit (e.g., r/freelance, r/forhire)
  • Indie Hackers
  • Facebook groups
  • Discord servers

Be helpful and show up consistently — clients prefer freelancers they trust.

5. Ask for Referrals from People You Know

Your first few clients can come from your own network. Friends, family, or classmates can connect you to people in need of your services.

Example message:

“Hey friends! I just started offering freelance graphic design services. If you or someone you know needs help with logos, banners, or social media graphics — feel free to reach out!”

6. Create Value-Driven Content

Content helps you show your expertise and attract the right audience.

  • Blog posts (e.g., common mistakes in branding)
  • YouTube tutorials (e.g., “How to write a great landing page”)
  • Instagram carousels (e.g., design tips)

When people find your content helpful, they’re more likely to hire you.

7. Partner with Other Freelancers

Freelancers often refer work to each other. Build genuine relationships with people in similar or complementary fields.

Example: A writer may refer you to their client who also needs design work.

Final Thoughts

Getting clients without relying on Upwork or Fiverr is not only possible — it’s often better. You’ll keep more of your income, set your own rules, and build stronger relationships. Stay consistent, provide value, and grow your network — success will follow.
